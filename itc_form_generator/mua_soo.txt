1.3 DEFINITIONS
A. AI – Analog Input
B. AO - Analog Output
C. DI - Discrete Input
D. DO - Discrete Output
E. Setpoint (SP) – The desired or target value that the system is trying to maintain.
F. Process Variable (PV) – Measured value of a particular part of a process which is being monitored or
controlled by the system. This could be Temperature, Pressure, Humidity, etc.
G. PID Control – Control system where the system continuously calculates the error value between the
desired setpoint (SP) and a measure process variable (PV) and applies correction based on proportional,
integral and derivative terms (denoted P, I and D respectively). The system shall use PID control unless
otherwise noted. Some processes may use PI control which will be identified in the SOO.
H. Direct Acting Loop – Control system where the controller output tends to increase as the PV increases. So,
if the PV is above SP, the controller output tends towards 100% output signal. If the PV is below SP, the
controller output tends towards 0%.
I. Reverse Acting Loop - Control system where the controller output tends to decrease as the PV increases.
So, if the PV is above SP, the controller output tends towards 0% output signal. If the PV is below SP, the
controller output tends towards 100%.

PART 3 - EXECUTION
3.1 GENERAL OVERVIEW
Each data center building has four Makeup Air Units (MUA). Each data center building phase consists of
two MUAs that shall operate as lead-lag units and are sized at N+1 for redundancy. Units will provide
100% outside, ventilation air to the FCA galleries (Data Hall & Network Suites) and provide heating only
via electric heating. Humidification shall be provided by centralized humidifiers – see Section 259510.
Manufacturer provided unitary controllers will provide operational control for the units. The unitary
controller will be connected to a nearby RIO Panel via Modbus RTU for monitoring and supervisory
purposes.
A. When enabled, the MUA will use its own packaged controls and instrumentation to condition the outside
air to meet the following setpoints as measured in the unit's supply air to the spaces.
1. Maintain supply air dry bulb temperature above 50 °F.
2. Maintain supply air relative humidity below 85%.
a. This will be accomplished by maintaining an offset of 5 °F between the supply air dry bulb
temperature and supply air dew point temperature.
B. Each MUA will contain the following components. See Drawing IC712 for controls schematic.
1. Major Equipment:
a. Supply Air Isolation Damper
b. Outside Air Isolation Damper
c. Supply Fan Array (VFD-driven Fan)
d. Electric Heating Coil Assembly (SCR-controlled)
e. Air Filter Bank with Differential Pressure Sensor
2. Major Instruments:
a. Supply Fan Static Pressure Switch
b. Supply Air Combination Temperature & Humidity Transmitter
c. Outside Air Combination Temperature & Humidity Transmitter
d. Supply Air Duct Pressure Transmitter
C. The following points shall be hardwired from the MUA controller to the associated Supervisory PLC panel
as shown in the drawings.
1. DO Enable/Disable Command
2. DI Run Status
3. DI Fault Status

3.3 MUA UNIT ENABLE/DISABLE
A. The MUA system is intended to run continuously unless shut down on smoke alarm (fire alarm system
interlock), Unit Failure, Utility Loss, or by the operators through SCADA. The operator can manually enable
or disable each MUA individually through SCADA. When a MUA is enabled, the unitary controller will first
send a signal to open its Intake Air Damper and Supply Air Isolation Damper. Once the dampers prove
open, the unitary controller will command the MUA to run. The BMS PLC/SCADA does not directly control
fan speed. The MUA fan array will be controlled to maintain a duct static pressure reading as defined
during Test and Balance (TAB) to maintain the design airflow.
B. MUA shall be programmed to rotate operation to equalize runtime.
C. When the MUA is disabled on an internal fault or is disabled through the PLC/SCADA, the heater will be
disabled, the fan array will be called to stop, then the MUA will close its Intake Air Damper & Supply Air
Isolation Damper.
D. The table below summarizes the state of several MUA components when the MUA is disabled, when the
component loses signal, and when the component loses power.

TABLE 1 – FAILURE STATES
Device Name                    Unit 'Off' Position/State    End Device Loss of Signal    End Device Loss of Power
Supply Air Isolation Damper    Closed                       Fail Closed                  Fail Closed
Outside Air Isolation Damper   Closed                       Fail Closed                  Fail Closed
MUA Supply Fan Array           Off                          Off                          Off

E. When the MUA is on, the MUA shall control to the following setpoints via its onboard controller:
1. Supply Air Low Temperature Setpoint
F. On receipt of any of the alerts listed below, a Unit Failure alert is raised and the MUA will shut down. Each
of these alerts shall be latching alerts requiring manual reset.
1. MUA Run Status Mismatch.
2. MUA Fault Status.

3.4 INTAKE AIR DAMPER & SUPPLY ISOLATION DAMPER
A. Critical set points
PARAMETER                                    SET POINT    TIME DELAY
Damper Actuator End Switch Mismatch Alert    N/A          Runtime + 60 seconds

B. Manual Operation
1. None.
C. Normal Operation
1. When the MUA is enabled, the damper shall be commanded open. The damper end switch shall be
interlocked to the VFD Enable input.
D. Failure Modes
1. Upon power failure the damper actuator fails (spring) closed.
2. Upon command signal loss the damper actuator closes.
E. Alerts and Faults
1. Once the MUA has been enabled and the damper commanded open, if a damper end switch does
not indicate the damper is in the opened position after the Damper Actuator End Switch Mismatch
Alert time delay has passed, an alert shall be generated.

3.5 SUPPLY FAN ARRAY (VFD DRIVEN)
A. Critical set points
PARAMETER                          SET POINT                TIME DELAY
Duct Static Pressure Setpoint      TBD at TAB               N/A
Min Fan Array Speed Setpoint       TBD at TAB               N/A
Max Fan Array Speed Setpoint       TBD at TAB               N/A
Fan Array Run Status Mismatch      N/A                      120 Seconds
Low Duct Static Pressure           50% of Pressure Setpoint 300 Seconds

B. Manual Operation
1. None.
C. Normal Operation
1. When the MUA is enabled and the damper end switches (Outside and Supply) are proved open, the
fan array will operate continuously. Upon loss of either damper end switch open proof, the supply
fan array shall be disabled.
2. The speed of the fan array will modulate between the Min Fan Array Speed setpoint and Max Fan
Speed Array setpoint in response to the Duct Static Pressure setpoint and filter loading.
D. Failure Modes
1. Upon power failure the supply fan array shuts off.
2. Upon command signal loss, the supply fan array shuts off.
E. Alerts and Faults
1. If the Supply Fan Array Run Status indicates Off for longer than the Fan Array Run Status Mismatch
time delay when the unit is enabled, an alert shall be generated.
2. If the Duct Static Pressure indicates it is below the Low Duct Static Pressure setpoint for longer than
the time delay when the Supply Fan Array Run Status indicates On, an alert shall be generated.
3. If the unit is Enabled and the High Discharge Pressure switch indicates on, an alert shall be
generated and the unit shall shutdown.

3.6 ELECTRIC HEATING COIL (SCR DRIVEN) BANK
A. Critical Setpoints
PARAMETER                                SET POINT    TIME DELAY
Supply Air Low Temperature Setpoint      50° F        N/A
Supply Air High Relative Humidity Setpoint 90% RH     N/A
High Humidity Offset                     5%           N/A

B. Manual Operation
1. None.
C. Normal Operation
1. Heating Mode
a. When the Unit is running and the Outside Air Dry Bulb Temperature is below the Supply Air
Low Temperature Setpoint, the electric heating SCR bank on the MUA will modulate to
maintain the Supply Air Temperature at the Supply Air Low Temperature Setpoint.
2. High Relative Humidity Mode
a. When the unit is running, and the Outside Air Relative Humidity is above the Supply Air High
Relative Humidity Setpoint, the electric heating SCR bank on the MUA will modulate to
maintain Supply Air Relative Humidity between the Supply Air High Relative Humidity Setpoint
and Supply Air High Relative Humidity Setpoint – High Humidity Offset. Once the Outside Air
Relative Humidity is below the Supply Air High Relative Humidity Setpoint – High Humidity
Offset, the MUA will return to normal operation.
3. Economization Mode
a. When the unit is running and the following conditions are met, the MUA electric heating SCR
bank will be locked out from operating.
1) Outside Air Dry Bulb Temperature is above the Supply Air Low Temperature Setpoint.
2) Outside Air Relative Humidity is below the Supply Air High Relative Humidity Setpoint –
High Humidity Offset.
D. Failure Modes
1. Upon power failure the electric heating SCR bank will not operate.
2. Upon command signal loss the electric heating SCR bank will not operate.
E. Alerts
1. Electric Heater Fail to Run.

3.8 UTILITY LOSS MODE
A. Momentary Disturbance (<5 seconds)
1. MUA is de-energized on loss of utility power and restarts upon restoration of utility power source.
B. Battery Operation Transition (between 5 and 45 seconds)
1. Upon loss of utility power, after 5 seconds, the MUA is Disabled by the BMS. MUA shall not run
while the site is on Generator power.
2. Upon restoration of Utility Power, the Enable signal to the MUA shall be automatically restored.
C. Controlled Shutdown (greater than 45 seconds)
1. Upon loss of utility power, after 5 seconds, the MUA is Disabled by the BMS. MUA shall not run
while the site is on Generator power.
2. Upon restoration of Utility Power, the Enable signal to the MUA shall be automatically restored.
